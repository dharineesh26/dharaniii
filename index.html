EXPECTED SKILLS

1.Programming Languages: Proficiency in languages like Python, Java, C++, or JavaScript is often required, depending on the job role.

2.Web Development: Knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as frameworks like React, Angular, or Vue.js, is crucial for web development roles.

3.Database Management: Understanding of database systems like MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, or SQL Server, including querying and database design.

4.Networking: Knowledge of network protocols, configurations, and troubleshooting skills are essential, especially for roles like network administrators or engineers.

5.Cybersecurity: Understanding of security principles, best practices, and tools for protecting systems and data from cyber threats.

6.Cloud Computing: Familiarity with cloud platforms like AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud, and knowledge of deploying, managing, and scaling applications in the cloud.

7.Operating Systems: Proficiency in Linux/Unix and Windows environments, including system administration tasks and shell scripting.

Remember that the IT landscape is constantly evolving, so staying updated with the latest technologies and trends in your area of interest is also important for career growth and staying competitive in the job
